The case for this island

Six people, one place, no compromises

Most islands make you pick a lane — beach trip, or culture trip, or party trip. This one doesn't. It's the rare place where a sunrise temple walk, a cliffside infinity pool, and a proper night out can all happen inside 48 hours, in three different micro-climates you can drive between before lunch.

  • Highlands for the early risers

    Mist, terraces, and stone temples before the day heats up — done by breakfast.

  • A cliff edge for the golden hour

    Limestone drops straight into the Indian Ocean — best watched with a drink in hand.

  • An island off the island

    A fast boat away, a whole other coastline of hidden coves nobody in the group has done yet.

  • Villas built for exactly six

    Private pool, a cook if you want one, and nobody splitting a two-person hotel room three ways.

The maths of travelling as a pack

What six gets you that two doesn't

Every one of these costs mostly stays flat whether it's two people or six — which means the more of you there are, the less each person pays for a noticeably better version of the same day.

CostJust a coupleThe six of youSaving
Villa, per person / night A 1BR villa alone runs ~₹6,500/night — ₹3,250 each. A 3–4BR pool villa with a proper living space runs ~₹14,000/night, split six ways. −₹900/night
Transfers & day trips Metered taxis or two-up scooters for every leg, booked separately. One private van and driver for the whole trip, on call for every transfer and tour. −₹650/day
Private dinners & a chef A villa chef or private beach dinner is close to a flat fee either way — expensive for two. The same flat fee, now split six ways — a private chef night becomes genuinely affordable. −60% pp
Tours & boat trips Standard per-head pricing, no leverage to negotiate. Six seats booked together routinely unlocks group rates, 10–15% off list price. ~₹500–700
The trip itself A quiet, low-key holiday. Three different travel instincts pulling the itinerary in three good directions — nobody's day looks the same twice. priceless
Before wheels up

Logistics & packing

Documents

  • Passport valid 6+ months past entry, 2 blank pages
  • Visa on arrival — carry small IDR/USD cash for it
  • Return flight proof & villa booking printouts
  • Travel insurance for all six
  • International driving permit if anyone's riding a scooter

Money & connectivity

  • Mix of cash (IDR) and cards — villas & tours often want cash
  • Local eSIM landed before departure, or grab one on arrival
  • Split a shared trip wallet/app for the group expenses
  • Notify banks of Indonesia travel dates

Pack for three micro-climates

  • Light rain shell — late September can still throw a shower
  • Reef-safe sunscreen, it's non-negotiable on the boat day
  • Something modest for temple visits (sarongs usually provided)
  • Real shoes for the rice terraces, not just slides
  • A dry bag for the fast-boat crossing
